Common English Research Nomenclature

Is your experiment in this graph? (Project 8 is not! It's not an acronym! There are too many acronyms. Many of them are terrible.)

You can also use this graph as a puzzle! In the smaller version, I've left all the acronyms blank for you to figure out---as well as all of the words that get used more than onces. You'll have to work your way in from the edges!. In the giant version, as you solve the experiment-acronym graph, you can fill in an acrostic on another mysterious graph, which in turn lets you figure out a cluephrase.

Footnote: I had this data and code on hand for a puzzle but I thought it'd be interesting to see in this form. I excluded (a) completely disconnected nodes ("Wilkinson Microwave Anisotropy Probe") (b) acronymic uses of non-alphabetic characters (K0TO, T2K, MINERνA) and less formally (c) things that are barely acronyms (XMASS) (d) things I couldn't find documentation of the name's meaning (LIBRA? TASSO?) and (e) things that technically have acronyms but where everyone uses a different name (my sense is that people say "Allen array" rather than "ATA"). Nonexistent (defunct, cancelled, proposed) projects and projects not famous enough to have Wikipedia pages are included irregularly.
